# OpenPGxLab Glossary

This glossary collects important terms used throughout OpenPGxLab.

## Active drug
A drug that is already in its pharmacologically active form when administered.

## Adverse drug reaction
A harmful or unwanted effect caused by a drug.

## Annotation
The process of adding biological, clinical, or technical meaning to a genetic variant or genotype.

## Bioinformatics
The use of computational methods to process, analyze, interpret, and manage biological data.

## CNV
Copy number variation. A gain or loss of genomic material that may affect gene function.

## Clinical decision support
Tools or systems that help clinicians make decisions using patient-specific information, including PGx results.

## Diplotype
The pair of haplotypes or star alleles assigned to a gene in one individual.

## Drug hypersensitivity
An abnormal immune-mediated reaction to a drug.

## Genotype
The genetic state observed at one or more loci in an individual.

## Haplotype
A set of genetic variants that are inherited together on the same chromosome.

## HLA
Human leukocyte antigen. A gene system involved in immune recognition and highly relevant in some drug hypersensitivity reactions.

## Metabolizer phenotype
A functional category describing expected metabolic activity for a drug-related gene, such as poor, intermediate, normal, rapid, or ultrarapid metabolizer.

## Pharmacodynamics
What the drug does to the body, including target interaction and biological response.

## Pharmacogenetics
The study of how one or a few genes influence drug response.

## Pharmacogenomics (PGx)
The study of how genetic variation influences drug efficacy, toxicity, dosing, and adverse reactions.

## Pharmacokinetics
What the body does to the drug, including absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion.

## Phenoconversion
A situation in which actual drug-metabolizing activity differs from genotype-predicted activity because of external factors such as drug-drug interactions.

## Phenotype
The observable or inferred functional consequence of a genotype, such as a metabolizer category.

## Prodrug
A drug that must be converted by the body into its active form.

## Short-read sequencing
Sequencing technology that generates relatively short DNA reads, commonly used in standard NGS workflows.

## Long-read sequencing
Sequencing technology that generates much longer DNA reads, which can help resolve phasing, structural variation, and complex loci.

## SNP array
A genotyping technology used to detect predefined variants at known positions in the genome.

## Star allele
A haplotype naming system commonly used in PGx genes, such as CYP2D6 or CYP2C19.

## Structural variant
A large genomic alteration such as deletion, duplication, inversion, or rearrangement.

## Variant
A difference in DNA sequence compared with a reference or between individuals.

## WES
Whole exome sequencing. Sequencing focused mainly on protein-coding regions of the genome.

## WGS
Whole genome sequencing. Sequencing of the full genome, including coding and noncoding regions.
